Assistant Professor of Theatre and Dance

The University of Texas at Austin

Research Interests

My research investigates the intersections of dance, technology, and cultural memory, focusing on how embodied knowledge is archived, transmitted, and reactivated through digital, material, and affective systems. I explore performance as a site of epistemological inquiry. Central to my work are projects such as Global Movement Research (GMR) and The Thinking Archive, which examine the transformation of archival practice through artificial embodied intelligence (AEI), motion capture, and ethical design. My broader interests include digital humanities, decolonial methodologies, AI and embodiment, cross-cultural movement analysis, and the creation of interactive archives that reconceive preservation as participation.
